# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curry Kitchen serves local customers in Oshawa and nearby areas who want reliable homemade Indian meals without cooking every day. Primary audiences are students, busy professionals, families, and first-time visitors comparing tiffin plans. The homepage also needs to convince the business owner/client that the React rebuild will feel more premium and easier to use than the current WordPress/WooCommerce site.

## Product Purpose

The website presents Curry Kitchen as a trustworthy homemade tiffin delivery service, guides visitors from appetite and weekly menu discovery into package selection, and previews the future ordering experience. Phase 1 is a high-fidelity static mock for design approval; success means the client can understand the brand direction, homepage narrative, package value, and future customer/admin flows without backend functionality.

## Brand Personality

Handcrafted, confident, and warm. The brand should feel artisan and food-focused, with enough polish to feel premium and modern. It should communicate homemade care without looking rustic, generic, or plugin-built.

## Anti-references

Avoid the cluttered WordPress/WooCommerce feel of the current site, generic restaurant templates, beige-heavy AI landing pages, random glassmorphism, repeated icon-card grids, weak stock-photo usage, and SaaS-style dashboard language on public marketing sections. Do not make the homepage feel like a tech startup, a generic cafe, or a plain ecommerce product list.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with appetite: food imagery and copy should make the service feel immediately desirable.
2. Make the ordering path obvious: every major section should help visitors understand menu, packages, customization, and delivery.
3. Balance homemade warmth with premium control: artisan typography and orange accents, anchored by black-and-white contrast.
4. Use motion purposefully: transitions should feel polished and guided, not decorative or distracting.
5. Keep client approval in mind: the homepage should tell a clear business story from hero to footer.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for public pages, visible focus states, semantic headings and landmarks, useful image alt text, keyboard-accessible navigation and CTAs, and reduced-motion alternatives for all nonessential animation. Avoid text over busy images unless contrast is explicitly protected.
